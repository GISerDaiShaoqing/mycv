The application of spatial analysis and spatial statistics in geographical, ecological and environmental research

 

Applications in geographical, ecological and environmental research

We used spatial analysis, spatial statistics and data mining in geographical, ecological and environmental research. Firstly, we used the Pearson correlation coefficient, generalized linear model, geographically weighted generalized linear regression model to analyze the impact of urban walking environment on robbery, snatch and theft crime (RST). Secondly, Regional soil quality issues arising from rapid urbanization have received extensive attention. Correlation, principle component analysis, cluster analysis and GeoDetector models were used to analyze the heavy metal sources in the soil accross an urbanization gradient. Thirdly, we used panel model to analyze the effects of command-and-control and market-oriented policy tools on China’s energy conservation and emissions reduction innovation. Forthly, some data mining technology was used to analyze urban spatial development pattern in beijing based on the big data of government. Finally, we developed a method to identify the urban ecological-production-living spaces and analyzed the high education staff’s refuge space under earthquake disater. These studies included criminal geography, heavy metal sources in soil, environmental economics, land use and disater, we used spatial analysis, spatial statistics and data mining in different topic researches. It’s very interesting.

Results

  • Influence of Walking Environment on Robbery, Snatch and Theft Crime in Urban Area, H City, China

Walking environment in urban

The visualization of correlation coefficient matrix and scatter plot

Spatial variation of regression coefficient of GWGLM

The results showed that the walking environment probably has a positive effect on the RST crime in H city, the greater walkability, the more RST. The effects of walking environment probably has spatial heterogeneity features both on RST and the residence probability of suspects.

  • Analysis of Heavy Metal Sources in the Soil of Riverbanks Across an Urbanization Gradient

The four groups (Cr-Ni-Cu, Cu-Zn-As-Sb, Cd and Pb) shared the same sources in the core urban region; five groups (Cr-Ni-Cu-Zn, As, Cd, Sb and Pb) in the suburbs and three groups (Cr-Ni, Cu-Zn-Cd-Sb-Pb and As) in the exurbs. GeoDetector methods not only validated the results of the three other methods, but also provided more possible impact factors. Besides the direct influences, the interaction effects among factors were quantified. Interactive combination with strong nonlinear increment changed from between-two-weak factors in the central region to between-strong-and-weak factors in the suburbs. In the exurbs, the stronger interaction effects were observed between strong and weak factors.

The spatial distribution of the heavy metal concentrations during the urbanization gradients

The inverse distance weighted (IDW) interpolated spatial patterns of Cd, Ni, Cu, Zn, As, Cd, Sb and Pb concentrations in the topsoil of the study area.

Numbers of nonlinear increase combinations in (a) urban core regions, (b) suburban regions, and © exurban regions. “Increase” means the comparison between the q value of interactions and the sum of two impact factors. “Increase 30–50%” low-level non-linear enhancement, “Increase 50–100%” means mid-level non-linear enhancement, “Increase >100%” means high-level non-linear enhancement

The combinations of interactive factors with nonlinear enhancements in the suburban area were fewer than in other urban gradient areas.

  • The identification of urban ecological-production-living spaces and urban resilience simulation optimization

We used multi-source data to identify the urban ecological-production-living spaces and provided some suggesstions for urban resilience simulation optimization. We developed some ArcGIS toolbox based on model builder and Python. Our results won the national third prize in the 2th big data supports spatial plannning and design competition.

  • The Optimization Model of High Education Staff’s Refuge Space Under Earthquake Disater

This is a report of fund by Fujian Normal University(cxxl-2014137). According to the spatial and temporal distribution of proposed model assumptions, in different time scales according to different locations of the evacuation route analysis. And the crowd under earthquake disaster evacuation of about 64.412m/min by using the MCE evaluation method and entropy method.
