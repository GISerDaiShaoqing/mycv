The environmental and healthy effects of urban blue and green space

 

1 Sponge city planning research in the view of landscape analysis

1 Introduction

In the new normal period of economic transition in China, The Government came up with the concept of the Sponge City, we have some different views of sponge city planning The research extracted the impervious surface from the non-NGI(natural green infrastructure ) landscape and used the precipitation-volumetric method to simulate waterlogging scenarios. We determined the area of rainwater submersion by simulation experiments, the change in the NGI landscape by overlaying images of the region, and the association between these features by data mining. Using the change in ecology indices, we ascertained the changing spatial patterns of the NGI landscape and used these findings to formulate suggestions for Sponge City planning in Fuzhou. Overall, our work combines three research themes, urban waterlogging, landscape ecology, and data mining, for a deep analysis of the relationship between the NGI landscape and storm runoff in an urban environment.

Our research results are inculded in the 2016 International Low Impact Development Conference Abstracts Proceedings and display at the 2016 International Low Impact Development Conference. Our research papers are included in the “Proceedings of 2016 International Low Impact Development Conference-China”. The Organizing Committee have negotiated the publication of conference papers as an American Society of Civil Engineers (ASCE) special Proceedings. The ASCE Proceedings is EI-indexed and is very widely recognized world-wide.

2 Highlights

(1) Submerged areas were characterized by high population densities and high levels of human activity.

(2) Spatial association rule mining revealed a strong association between areas that were converted from NGI landscape and areas that were submerged in our simulations.

(3) Combination of urban waterlogging, landscape ecology and data mining.

2 How green space influence the fine particulate matter and urban heat island effect

1 Introduction

The spatial distribution of green space will influence the fine particulate matter, land surface temperature, and other urban ecological process. The influence include direct and indirect effects. How to quantify the relationship between green space and environmental effects is so important. We combine ground observations, remote sensing and spatial statistics models to determine it. The results can provide some suggestions for policy makers.

Our research results published in some international journals and won some awards in academic competitions.

The excellence award in the 5th “Share Cup” Science and Technology Resources Sharing Service Innovation Competition Competition.

2 Highlights

Using geodetector model to explore the direct and indirect relationship between the green space and environmental effects(fine particulate matter, urban heat island effect and so on).
